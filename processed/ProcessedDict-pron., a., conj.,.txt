That>>|<<pron., a., conj., &amp; adv.>>|<<As a demonstrative pronoun (pl. Those), that usually points out, or refers to, a person or thing previously mentioned, or supposed to be understood. That, as a demonstrative, may precede the noun to which it refers; as, that which he has said is true; those in the basket are good apples.
That>>|<<pron., a., conj., &amp; adv.>>|<<As an adjective, that has the same demonstrative force as the pronoun, but is followed by a noun.
That>>|<<pron., a., conj., &amp; adv.>>|<<As a relative pronoun, that is equivalent to who or which, serving to point out, and make definite, a person or thing spoken of, or alluded to, before, and may be either singular or plural.
That>>|<<pron., a., conj., &amp; adv.>>|<<As a conjunction, that retains much of its force as a demonstrative pronoun.
That>>|<<pron., a., conj., &amp; adv.>>|<<To introduce a clause employed as the object of the preceding verb, or as the subject or predicate nominative of a verb.
That>>|<<pron., a., conj., &amp; adv.>>|<<To introduce, a reason or cause; -- equivalent to for that, in that, for the reason that, because.
That>>|<<pron., a., conj., &amp; adv.>>|<<To introduce a purpose; -- usually followed by may, or might, and frequently preceded by so, in order, to the end, etc.
That>>|<<pron., a., conj., &amp; adv.>>|<<To introduce a consequence, result, or effect; -- usually preceded by so or such, sometimes by that.
That>>|<<pron., a., conj., &amp; adv.>>|<<In an elliptical sentence to introduce a dependent sentence expressing a wish, or a cause of surprise, indignation, or the like.
That>>|<<pron., a., conj., &amp; adv.>>|<<As adverb: To such a degree; so; as, he was that frightened he could say nothing.
